Bio

   My name is John M. Hofrichter, a senior at the University of Pittsburgh. I grew up in a town just south of the city, where I lived with my parents and two older sisters. The majority of my extended family is from the Pittsburgh area, with a few scattered across the globe. I attended Canon McMillan high school, and went on to Pitt, mostly because of my exposure from family alumni.

   In late high school I found that I was fairly skilled working with computers, especially programming, leading to current Comp Sci track. I have taken many upper level courses (listed elsewhere on the site), and, despite my initial feelings, have found that programming is a very entertaining way to pass the time. In particular, I enjoy lower level system programming, with an emphasis on networks, and Cryptography. I spent three semesters working as a software developer for Ansys Inc. in my home town, and can truly see a rewarding future in the field.

   In terms of free time, I usually occupy myself with several hobbies. A few years ago, I discovered the university climbing wall and fell in love. I went on to work at the wall as an instructor, and still do today. The majority of my climbing has been done in the tri-state area, the only real exception being several summits of Seneca Rocks, in central West Virginia. I plan on exploring more exotic locations once I have means to do so. My other main hobby is woodworking. I have created several objects from furniture to carved crests, and have found that creating an object on your own power is extremely rewarding.

   The majority of my time is pent either working, exploring one of my hobbies, or hanging out with friends. This Summer I will be moving to the Baltimore suburbs for an internship, but will return to Pitt for another semester in the fall. After that time, I look forward to whatever the future holds!
